▲ Priority scoring
Every prospect gets a score from 0–100 built from three factors:

State weight (50 pts max)
MA scores highest (50 pts). VA lowest (6 pts). PA and NY are tied at priority 3.

Entity type (35 pts max)
Cultivators get the full 35 pts. Processors get 23 pts. Testing labs get 12 pts.

License status (15 pts max)
Active: +15 pts   Pending: +5 pts   Expired/unknown: +0 pts

A MA cultivator with an active license scores 100. A VA testing lab with an expired license scores ~18.
⇄ Deduplication
When you import from multiple sources, the same business often appears more than once.

Automatic blocking
Any record with the same business name + state as an existing entry is blocked on import. Catches exact duplicates across all sources.

Fuzzy matching (review phase)
Claude will scan for near-matches: "Green Leaf Farms LLC" vs "Green Leaf Farm", abbreviations, DBA names, and address duplicates with different business names.

Best data wins
When duplicates are merged, the record with the most complete contact info is kept as the primary.
⚑ Entity classification
State databases use wildly inconsistent license category names. The app normalizes them automatically:

Cultivator — cultivat, grow, farm, plant, flower, Tier 1
Processor — process, manufactur, extract, infus, MIP, Tier 2
Testing lab — test, lab, analy, certif

During review, Claude flags any record where the classification looks wrong based on the business name or address, and asks you to confirm before changing it.
⌖ Distance filtering
All distances are measured from Easthampton, MA using the Haversine formula (straight-line, not driving distance).

Exact — scraped records request lat/lng from the AI. Distance is precise to the address.

Estimated (~) — Excel or Google Maps records without coordinates use the geographic center of their state. Can be off by 50–100 miles for large states like NY and PA. A tilde marks estimated distances in the table.

Use the radius filter in the Prospect list tab to narrow to a specific distance from Easthampton.
⚲ Address validation (review phase)
A licensed address is not always the grow site. During full review, Claude flags:

• Law firm or registered agent addresses (downtown, suite numbers)
• Corporate HQ that doesn't match the license city
• PO Box addresses with no physical location
• Addresses shared across multiple records (same registered agent)

Flagged records get a note added. You decide whether to keep, update, or remove.
⚡ Enrichment (review phase)
For high-priority records (MA and NJ cultivators first), Claude web-searches to fill missing data:

• Phone & email — from company website, LinkedIn, state filings
• Contact name & title — Cultivation Director, COO, Operations Manager (not the license attorney)
• Facility confirmation — verifying the address is an actual grow site
• Operational status — some licensed businesses are not yet operating

Enrichment runs in batches. You review and approve before anything is saved.
